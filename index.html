Guillermo Droppelmann, PhD

Guillermo Droppelmann is a Clinical Researcher and PhD in Health Sciences (Summa Cum Laude) with postdoctoral training in Industrial Engineering. He is a scientific leader bridging clinical practice and data science, with a research focus on machine and deep learning applied to musculoskeletal imaging and diagnostics. He is the author of peer-reviewed publications and book chapters, principal investigator on competitive R&D grants, and a Harvard-awarded teaching collaborator.

He currently serves as Deputy Head of Research at Clínica MEDS (Santiago, Chile) and as Lecturer in the PhD Program in Industrial Engineering at Pontificia Universidad Católica de Valparaíso (PUCV). He is currently pursuing his second doctoral degree — a PhD in Bioengineering at Hospital Italiano de Buenos Aires, working on multimodal AI modeling of ECG signals and biomarkers for the detection of chemotherapy-induced cardiotoxicity in cancer patients.

Research areas

  • Machine learning and deep learning applied to medical imaging
  • Musculoskeletal diagnostics (ultrasound and MRI)
  • AI-assisted diagnosis and clinical decision support

Current research line: detection of electrocardiographic signals and biomedical markers for the identification of comorbidities and disease burden in cancer patients.
